<p><p>Audio conferencing endpoints are devices used to facilitate communication during meetings. Tabletop endpoints are standalone devices placed on a table and are suitable for small to medium-sized meeting rooms. Installed endpoints, on the other hand, are integrated into the room's audiovisual system and are typically used in larger meeting spaces or boardrooms. Both types of endpoints provide high-quality audio for participants to engage in remote meetings effectively, but they differ in terms of size, installation, and suitability for different meeting environments.</p></p>
