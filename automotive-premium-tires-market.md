<p><p>Bias tires are constructed with multiple layers of rubber-coated fabric crisscrossed at a 30 to 40-degree angle. They are designed for heavy-duty applications and typically have a stiffer sidewall, providing stability and strength. Radial tires, on the other hand, have layers of cords running perpendicular to the direction of travel, resulting in a smoother ride and better fuel efficiency. They are commonly used in passenger vehicles and offer improved traction and cornering capabilities. Both types serve different purposes and cater to specific needs in the automotive premium tires market.</p></p>
