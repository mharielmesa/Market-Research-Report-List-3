<p><ul><li>Intrinsic Photoresistor</li><li>Extrinsic Photoresistor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Light dependent photoresistors are categorized into two main types: intrinsic and extrinsic. Intrinsic photoresistors are made from pure semiconductor materials and rely on the movement of electrons within the material to change resistance in response to light. Extrinsic photoresistors, on the other hand, are doped with impurities to enhance their light sensitivity. These impurities create additional energy levels in the material, allowing for a more controlled response to light. Both types of photoresistors play a crucial role in various applications where light detection and control are needed.</p></p>
