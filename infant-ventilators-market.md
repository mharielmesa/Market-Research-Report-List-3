<p><ul><li>Continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP)</li><li>Noninvasive positive pressure ventilation (NIPPV)</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Infant ventilators are used to provide respiratory support to infants with breathing difficulties. Continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) delivers a continuous flow of air to keep the airways open, while noninvasive positive pressure ventilation (NIPPV) provides breathing support without the need for an invasive tube. Other types of infant ventilators may include high-frequency oscillatory ventilation (HFOV) and volume targeted ventilation. These different types of ventilators cater to the varying needs of infants, offering effective respiratory support and improving outcomes for neonatal patients.</p></p>
