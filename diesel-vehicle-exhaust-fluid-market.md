<p><p>Diesel Vehicle Exhaust Fluid (DEF) is a solution made up of urea and deionized water that is sprayed into the exhaust stream of diesel vehicles to reduce harmful nitrogen oxide emissions. The market growth is driven by the increasing stringency of emission regulations worldwide, which is pushing vehicle manufacturers to adopt DEF systems in their vehicles. This technology relies on DEF to reduce emissions, and it is becoming more common in new diesel vehicles to meet stringent emission standards.
