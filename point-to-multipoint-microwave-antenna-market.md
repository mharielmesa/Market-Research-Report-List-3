<p><p>Point-to-multipoint microwave antennas are divided into three main types: parabolic antennas, flat panel antennas, and other designs. Parabolic antennas are the most common type and feature a curved dish to focus the signal in a specific direction. Flat panel antennas are more compact and offer a lower profile option for urban environments. Other designs may include sector antennas or horn antennas, each tailored for specific use cases depending on factors such as coverage area, distance, and signal strength requirements.</p></p>
