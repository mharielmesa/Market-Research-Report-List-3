Ultrafiltration involves separating large molecules from smaller ones, while Microfiltration is used for removing suspended solids and bacteria. Nanofiltration, on the other hand, is a more selective process that separates ions and organic molecules. These technologies are essential in industries such as water treatment, pharmaceuticals, and food processing, where precise separation of components is required for product quality and efficiency.</p></p>
