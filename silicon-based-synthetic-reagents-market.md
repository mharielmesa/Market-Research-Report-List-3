<p><p>Silicon-based synthetic reagents market is divided into three main types: Peterson olefinations, cyanosilylations, and cross-coupling reactions. Peterson olefinations involve the conversion of carbonyl compounds to olefins using silicon-based reagents. Cyanosilylations refer to the addition of cyanide groups to silicon-containing compounds. Cross-coupling reactions involve the coupling of two different silicon-containing compounds to create new chemical entities. These types of reactions offer efficient ways to synthesize complex organic molecules and are widely utilized in the pharmaceutical, agrochemical, and materials industries.</p></p>
