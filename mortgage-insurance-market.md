<p><p>Mortgage insurance is a type of insurance that protects the lender in case the borrower defaults on their loan. There are three types of mortgage insurance markets: Borrower-Paid Mortgage Insurance, where the borrower pays premiums on top of their monthly mortgage payments; Lender-Paid Mortgage Insurance, where the lender pays the premiums in exchange for a higher interest rate; and FHA Mortgage Insurance, which is provided by the Federal Housing Administration for borrowers who have a smaller down payment. Each type of insurance has its own benefits and drawbacks for both borrowers and lenders.</p></p>
