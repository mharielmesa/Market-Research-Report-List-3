Preventive vaccines are designed to prevent individuals from contracting infectious diseases by stimulating the immune system to produce antibodies against specific pathogens. On the other hand, therapeutic vaccines are used to treat individuals who are already infected with a certain disease by helping the immune system target and eliminate the pathogen. Both types play a crucial role in controlling and managing infectious diseases worldwide.</p></p>
