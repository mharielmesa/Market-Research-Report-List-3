<p><p>Organic potato starch is available in two main types: native and modified. Native potato starch is in its natural form, without any chemical alterations, making it suitable for organic food products. On the other hand, modified potato starch undergoes a process that changes its properties such as viscosity and stability, making it more versatile for various food and industrial applications. Both types have their own market demand, with native starch often preferred for organic and clean-label products, while modified starch is popular for its functional benefits.</p></p>
