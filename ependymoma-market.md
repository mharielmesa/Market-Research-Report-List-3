<p><p>Ependymomas are classified into different types based on their characteristics and aggressiveness. Myxopapillary ependymomas are slow-growing tumors found in the lower part of the spinal cord, while subependymomas are typically non-cancerous and are often incidental findings. Classic ependymomas are the most common type, occurring in both children and adults, and may be slow-growing or more aggressive. Anaplastic ependymomas are the most aggressive type, with a higher likelihood of spreading to other parts of the central nervous system.</p></p>
