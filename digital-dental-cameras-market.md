<p><p>Digital dental cameras are used in the dental industry to capture high-quality images of the patient's teeth and oral cavity. There are two main types of digital dental cameras: intraoral and extraoral cameras. Intraoral cameras are used to take close-up images inside the mouth, providing detailed views of individual teeth and oral structures. Extraoral cameras, on the other hand, are used to capture larger images of the patient's entire face or specific areas of the mouth from a distance. Both types of cameras play a crucial role in diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient education in the dental field.</p></p>
