<p><p>Aircraft washing trucks come in two main types: self-propelled and towed. Self-propelled trucks are equipped with their own engine and are able to move independently around the airport apron. They offer more flexibility and maneuverability in reaching different aircraft in need of washing. Towed trucks, on the other hand, are connected to a towing vehicle and are pulled around the airport. While they may be less versatile than self-propelled trucks, they are typically more cost-effective and easier to operate for smaller airports or facilities.</p></p>
